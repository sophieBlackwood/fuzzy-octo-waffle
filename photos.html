Photo Corner

by Sophie Blackwood

I love taking photos. Sometimes it's just for remembering times, dates and events. Other times it is because I am just struck by the beauty of nature or the things around me. They say to see what someone is scared of losing, you just have to look at what they photograph.

Taken at work, the sunlight filtering on the books in the early evening always seems to bring warmth and beauty.

Sometimes I leave seminary just in time to catch beautiful sunrises. These always help the day start right.

I sprinted around this field faster than a footballer to get this shot, but I love it and how it shows love and support.

Another beautiful sunrise, the colors in the sky are at times something that is hard to even show in photos.

The sky is so beautiful. The cotton candy clouds this morning were worth a photo to remember the tranquility.

While sitting atop a mountain of straw bales, I witnessed the beautiful sunset with friends.

When someone sticks a camera in your face, you're not quite sure what to do. Smile? Frown? Ignore it? That's why you just take the photos.

The rainbow outside work brought a smile to our faces as it countered the dreary day and stormy skies of the day.

The temple will never not look beautiful to me. I could touch it and even still I would swear to you that it is to gorgeous to touch. It is majestic.

Lyndee was a fantastic girl, taken too young. Visiting her grave and keeping it decorated as I live the life she never got is bittersweet to me.

I don't like my hair down but the lighting of the computer screen illuminated my face in a good way which turned out well. I am not photogenic.

The beautiful peace of the temple and the cutting difference of it and the sky is unique. Photographing the temple never gets old.

The wind, while annoying, keeps the water fluid and moving. The sound of the waves is really peaceful when I have a tulmultous mind and can't stop thinking.

Flowers are so pretty, so fragile but every one is unique. I think people should recieve flowers more often, they brighten a room and are so diverse.

The clouds tell stories of their own, creating visual interest and diversity in this picture. Laying in the hammock outside, I knew I had to take a picture.

Even from afar the temple just seems to stand out. Even with the fences that used to keep us far away, the special presence of a building like this has never decreased.

Isn't it fascinating how sun and clouds can create so many different pictures? Why do we look down when we could be looking up. At all this beauty. Even the clairty of a simple blue sky holds majesty.

The lights of the temple illuminate the beautiful stained glass art. I have yet to decide if I like going during the day or at night more. Both are beautiful.

The sky set on fire allows for breathtaking views. It is simply unique and divine in ways that a normal blue sky cannot.
